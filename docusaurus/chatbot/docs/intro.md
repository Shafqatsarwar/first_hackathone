# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Welcome to the Future of AI

The future of work will be a partnership between people, intelligent agents (AI software), and robots. This shift won't necessarily eliminate jobs but will change what humans do, leading to a massive demand for new skills.

This textbook introduces **Physical AI**—AI systems that function in reality and comprehend physical laws. You'll learn to design, simulate, and deploy humanoid robots capable of natural human interactions using ROS 2, Gazebo, and NVIDIA Isaac.

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI represents a significant transition from AI models confined to digital environments to **embodied intelligence** that operates in physical space. Unlike traditional AI that processes data in the abstract, Physical AI must understand and interact with the physical world.

Key aspects of Physical AI include:
- Understanding physical laws (gravity, friction, momentum)
- Processing sensor data from the real world
- Controlling actuators to interact with physical objects
- Dealing with uncertainty and noise in real-world data
- Operating under real-time constraints

## Why Humanoid Robots?

Humanoid robots are poised to excel in our human-centered world because they:
- Share our physical form, allowing them to navigate human environments
- Can be trained with abundant data from interacting in human environments
- Enable intuitive human-robot interaction
- Leverage our understanding of human movement and behavior

## Course Structure

This course is organized into four modules:

### Module 1: The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)
Learn the middleware that connects all robot components, including nodes, topics, services, and how to bridge Python agents to ROS controllers.

### Module 2: The Digital Twin (Gazebo & Unity)
Explore physics simulation and environment building, including simulating sensors like LiDAR and cameras.

### Module 3: The AI-Robot Brain (NVIDIA Isaac™)
Discover advanced perception and training with hardware-accelerated VSLAM and navigation systems.

### Module 4: Vision-Language-Action (VLA)
Integrate voice recognition and LLMs to enable robots to understand and execute natural language commands.
